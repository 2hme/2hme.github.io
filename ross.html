Problem:

Tinder has been abused by many users, which downgrade the effectiveness of match making in its platform. To maximize the possibility of matching with girls/guys, it’s one’s best interest to always swipe right, which “likes” every single one you see without knowing them.

Core Solution:

Based on Tinder model: when first started, one user would be given a certain amount of free roses (eg. 99). When you swipe right to like someone. It means you will spend one rose. One can swipe right till he spends all the roses. Then:

a. He/she can wait a day, and get small amount of free roses (eg. 1).

b. Or he/she can choose to purchase roses with dollars.

This solution imply opportunity cost for the user which encourage them to make choices more carefully, thus result in a better effectiveness.
